Thank you for this comprehensive request. The clinical picture is highly evocative of newly diagnosed heart failure on a high-CV-risk profile. My recommendations:
1 · Provisional diagnosis
De novo heart failure — most likely HFpEF (preserved ejection fraction) given age, female sex, T2D, HTA and obesity. Underlying ischemic heart disease must be excluded given LVH on ECG, family history of premature MI and uncontrolled CV risk factors.
2 · To be done now — by you (GP)
Lab tests (today / within 48h)
  • NT-proBNP — gold-standard biomarker to confirm HF
  • Full lab panel: electrolytes (Na, K, Mg), creatinine + eGFR, ferritin, TSH, fasting glucose, HbA1c, complete lipid panel, CBC, CRP
Treatment to start tomorrow morning
  • Furosemide 40 mg — 1 tablet in the morning (loop diuretic, fluid overload)
  • Ramipril 2.5 mg — 1 tablet in the morning (ACE inhibitor; replaces Amlodipine, I will titrate up at the in-person visit)
  • Continue Atorvastatin but aim for LDL < 55 mg/dL in this very-high-risk profile
  • Continue Aspirin 75 mg and Metformin
  • Lifestyle: salt < 5 g/day, fluid < 1.5 L/day, smoking cessation (offer nicotine replacement)
3 · To be done at the in-person cardiology consultation — by me
  • Transthoracic echocardiography (TTE) — done in my office
  • 24h Holter ECG — fitted at the visit, to rule out paroxysmal AF (palpitations reported)
  • Coronary CT angiography or stress imaging — scheduled if TTE shows LV dysfunction or wall-motion abnormality
  • Therapeutic refinement based on TTE: titrate Ramipril, introduce Bisoprolol 2.5 mg if appropriate, beta-blocker titration
In-person cardiology consultation needed within 1 week (brought forward from 2 weeks given the imaging + Holter to do).
My secretary will contact Mrs ADAM today to book the slot.
Daily weight monitoring at home (alert if +1 kg/day or +2 kg/3d). Return to ED if rapid worsening dyspnea or any chest pain.
